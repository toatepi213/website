Te Mana o te Ira Tangata: Strengthening Gender Equality in Aotearoa

Gender equality brings numerous benefits to individuals, communities, and society as a whole. Here are some of the key benefits: Social Justice and Fairness: Gender equality promotes fairness and social justice by ensuring that all individuals, regardless of gender, have equal opportunities and rights. Improved Economic Growth: Countries with greater gender equality tend to have stronger economic growth. This is because women's participation in the workforce enhances productivity and contributes to overall economic development. Enhanced Creativity and Innovation: Diversity in perspectives fosters creativity and innovation. Gender equality ensures that diverse viewpoints are considered, leading to better problem-solving and decision-making. Better Quality of Life: Gender equality is linked to improved health outcomes, education, and overall quality of life for individuals. It enables people to reach their full potential and live fulfilling lives. Reduced Poverty: Empowering women economically through gender equality measures helps lift families and communities out of poverty. Women tend to invest more in their families' well-being, such as education and healthcare. Stronger Governance and Democracy: Gender-balanced representation in politics and leadership roles leads to more inclusive governance and better policy outcomes that reflect the needs of all members of society.

We Can Make a Difference

  1. Gender mainstreaming integrates gender concerns into policy analysis, formulation, and monitoring.
  2. It is a process and a means to achieve gender equality, not a goal in itself.
  3. The purpose is to promote gender equality and empower women in population and development activities.
  4. It involves addressing both the condition and position of women and men in society.
  5. Gender mainstreaming strengthens the legitimacy of gender equality values by tackling disparities in:
    • Division of labour between men and women
    • Access to and control over resources
    • Access to services, information, and opportunities
    • Distribution of power and decision-making
  6. UNFPA uses gender mainstreaming as the primary method to achieve gender equality, equity, and women's empowerment commitments from the International Conference on Population and Development.
